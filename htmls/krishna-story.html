The divine birth of Lord Krishna in Mathura.

Birth of Lord Krishna

The story of Lord Krishna's birth unfolds in the ancient kingdom of Mathura, ruled by the oppressive King Kamsa. Unable to bear the injustice, Lord Vishnu accepted the plea to reincarnate and defeat the tyrannical forces.

One fateful night, during the auspicious month of Shravana, Devaki gave birth to her eighth child‚ÄîLord Krishna. In a miraculous turn of events, the prison doors swung open, and Vasudeva carried the newborn Krishna across the Yamuna River, which miraculously parted to allow him safe passage.

The Transfer to Gokul

Vasudeva transported the infant Krishna to Nanda and Yashoda in Gokul, where he would grow up under their loving care, away from the dangers of Mathura. His upbringing was marked by divine leelas and miraculous powers.

Krishna's playful childhood in Gokul, stealing butter and charming hearts.

Krishna's Childhood Antics

Krishna's pastimes of drinking His mother's breast milk gradually stopped. Soon, He walked on His lotus feet and began stealing butter. His mischievous acts brought unlimited bliss to all who witnessed them.

One day, while no one was looking, Krishna began His mischief of stealing fresh butter. Mother Yasoda watched from a hidden place and heard His sweet broken words with great relish.

The Moon's Mystery

One night, Krishna pointed to the full moon and cried, "I want that freshly churned condensed butter!" The gopis tried various explanations, but Krishna insisted. Finally, Mother Yasoda gently explained the story of the Ksira-sagara and why the moon had spots of poison.

Krishna subdues the serpent Kaliya in the Yamuna river.

The Tale of Kaliya Naag

A fearsome serpent named Kaliya had poisoned the waters of Yamuna, spreading terror among the residents. When Krishna's ball accidentally fell into the river, He plunged in to retrieve it.

Kaliya emerged with its multiple hoods and coiled around Krishna. However, Krishna expanded His size, loosened the grip, and began dancing on the serpent's hoods with unmatched grace. As Krishna danced, divine melodies enchanted everyone, and the gods showered flowers from the sky.

In a compassionate gesture, Krishna chose not to harm Kaliya but expelled him from the Yamuna, imprinting His divine footprints on the serpent's head for protection. The purified Yamuna flowed serenely once again through Vrindavan.

Krishna lifts Govardhan Hill to protect villagers from Indra's wrath.

The Govardhan Leela

When the villagers decided not to worship Lord Indra following Krishna's advice, the furious King of Heaven sent devastating rains to flood Vrindavan.

Krishna lifted the entire Govardhan Hill with His little finger and held it like an umbrella. For seven days, all the inhabitants stayed safely under the hill, protected from the terrible rains. The hill rested perfectly balanced on Krishna's finger.

Stunned by these events, Indra called back the clouds. Krishna gently placed the hill back and the villagers celebrated. This shattered Indra's false pride, and he came to Krishna seeking forgiveness.

Krishna guides Arjuna in the battlefield of Kurukshetra, delivering the Bhagavad Gita.

The Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita, or the song of God, was revealed by Lord Krishna to Arjuna on the threshold of the epic war of Mahabharata. This sacred dialogue took place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra between two sets of cousins‚Äîthe Kauravas and the Pandavas.

Through this divine discourse, Krishna imparted timeless wisdom about dharma, karma, devotion, and the nature of reality, guiding not just Arjuna but all of humanity toward spiritual enlightenment.
